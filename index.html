Earless seal

These curious animals show many signs of complex cognition. They employ impressive means to find food and escape danger,
and have even been known to push drowning pets to safety.

  • Scientific name: Pinnipeds otaridae
  • Average Length: Between 1m. and 3m.
  • Average Lifespan: 25 - 30 years
  • Habitat: Mainly in the Arctic and Antarctic but also in some areas of the tropics

The earless seals or true seals are one of the three main groups of mammals within the seal lineage, Pinnipedia.
They are sometimes called crawling seals to distinguish them from the fur seals and sea lions. Seals mainly live in the water.
Because they can spend months at sea, they can sleep underwater.
